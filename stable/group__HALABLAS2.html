Detailed Description

BLAS Level 2
Level 2 operations are defines as those that include a single matrix and one or more vectors. Examples of such operations are matrix vector product and triangular solve.

Wrapper to BLAS general matrix-vector multiply xgemv().

Computes \( y = \alpha op(A) x + \beta y \), where op is either identity or, conjugate or real transpose operators.

  • trans must be lower or upper case N (identity), T (transpose), or C (conjugate transpose), indicates op
  • A must be at least N by lda
  • lda is the leading dimension of A, minimum M
  • incx and incy are strides of the entries of x and y
  • if beta is zero, y is a pure output and will be resized if the size is not sufficient

Overloads:

hala::gemv(trans, M, N, alpha, A, x, beta, y, lda = -1, incx = 1, incy = 1);
hala::gemv(engine, trans, M, N, alpha, A, lda, x, incx, beta, y, incy);
hala::gemv(engine, trans, M, N, alpha, A, x, beta, y, lda = -1, incx = 1, incy = 1);

The increments default to 1 and the leading dimension is replaced by the minimum acceptable (i.e., the matrix has no padding).

Wrapper to BLAS symmetric or Hermitian matrix-vector multiply xsymv().

Computes \( y = \alpha A x + \beta y \), where A is either symmetric or conjugate-symmetric (Hermitian).

  • uplo is lower/upper case U/L, indicates whether to use the upper (U) or lower (L) part of A
  • A must be at least N by lda
  • lda is the leading dimension of A, minimum N
  • incx and incy are strides of the entries of x and y
  • if beta is zero, y is a pure output and will be resized if the size is not sufficient

Overloads:

hala::symv(uplo, N, alpha, A, x, beta, y, lda = -1, incx = 1, incy = 1);

hala::hemv(engine, uplo, N, alpha, A, x, beta, y, lda = -1, incx = 1, incy = 1);

The increments default to 1 and the leading dimension is replaced by the minimum acceptable (i.e., the matrix has no padding). The symv() and hemv() variants differ only in the complex case, where A is assumed to be symmetric or conjugate symmetric (Hermitian).

Wrapper to BLAS triangular matrix-vector multiply xtrmv().

Computes \( x = op(A) x \), where op is either identity or, conjugate or real transpose operators. Here A is assumed to be upper or lower triangular matrix with either unit or non-unit diagonal.

  • uplo must lower or upper case U or L, indicates whether to work with the upper (U) or lower (L) part of A
  • trans must be lower or upper case N (identity), T (transpose), or C (conjugate transpose), indicates op
  • diag must be lower or upper case U or N, indicates whether A has unit (U) or non-unit (N) diagonal
  • only the array elements of A indicated by uplo and diag are accessed, the rest is not read
  • lda is the leading dimension of A (minimum N) and incx is the stride of vector entries

Overloads:

hala::trmv(uplo, trans, diag, N, A, x, lda = -1, incx = 1);
hala::trmv(engine, uplo, trans, diag, N, A, lda, x, incx);
hala::trmv(engine, uplo, trans, diag, N, A, x, lda = -1, incx = 1);

The increment defaults to 1 and the leading dimension is replaced by the minimum acceptable (i.e., the matrix has no padding).

Wrapper to BLAS triangular matrix-vector solve xtrsv().

Solves \( op(A) x = b \), where op is either identity or, conjugate or real transpose operators. Here A is assumed to be upper or lower triangular matrix with either unit or non-unit diagonal.

  • uplo must lower or upper case U or L, indicates whether to work with the upper (U) or lower (L) part of A
  • trans must be lower or upper case N (identity), T (transpose), or C (conjugate transpose), indicates op
  • diag must be lower or upper case U or N, indicates whether A has unit (U) or non-unit (N) diagonal
  • only the array elements of A indicated by uplo and diag are accessed, the rest is not read
  • x must have N active entries, where the active entries lay in a stride of incx
  • on entry x is the right hand side of the equation, on exit x is the solution
  • lda is the leading dimension of A (minimum N)

Overloads:

hala::trsv(uplo, trans, diag, N, A, x, lda = -1, incx = 1);
hala::trsv(engine, uplo, trans, diag, N, A, lda, x, incx);
hala::trsv(engine, uplo, trans, diag, N, A, x, lda = -1, incx = 1);

The increment defaults to 1 and the leading dimension is replaced by the minimum acceptable (i.e., the matrix has no padding).

Wrapper to BLAS general rank-1 update xgerx().

Computes \( A = A + \alpha x y^T \) with either conjugate or non-conjugate transpose.

  • conjugate indicates whether to consider real or complex transpose (defaults to complex)
  • A is an M by N matrix with leading dimension lda (minimum M)
  • x and y are vectors with entries in strides incx and incy
  • the number of active entries in x and y must be M and N respectively

Overloads:

hala::ger(M, N, alpha, x, y, A, incx = 1, incy = 1, lda = -1);
hala::geru(M, N, alpha, x, y, A, incx = 1, incy = 1, lda = -1);
hala::ger(engine, M, N, alpha, x, incx, y, incy, A, lda);
hala::geru(engine, M, N, alpha, x, incx, y, incy, A, lda);
hala::ger(engine, M, N, alpha, x, y, A, incx = 1, incy = 1, lda = -1);
hala::geru(engine, M, N, alpha, x, y, A, incx = 1, incy = 1, lda = -1);

The increment defaults to 1 and the leading dimension is replaced by the minimum acceptable. The geru() variant applies the transpose without conjugation.
